Microformats and Microdata

A Brief Overview

What they are

  • mark up content with “meta” information
  • meta information identifies content as being of a particular type of data
    • Product
    • Person
    • Event

Why use them?

  • make content machine readable
  • better indexing for SEO

Flavors

  • Microdata
  • Microformats
  • RDFa*



* included for completeness

Microdata

  • proposed by major search engine vendors
  • add attributes to HTML markup


Example

Before Microdata


<div>
 <h1>Avatar</h1>
 <span>Director: James Cameron (born August 16, 1954)</span>
 <span>Science fiction</span>
 <a href="../movies/avatar-theatrical-trailer.html">Trailer</a>
</div>


After Microdata


<div itemscope itemtype ="http://schema.org/Movie">
  <h1 itemprop="name"&g;Avatar</h1>
  <div itemprop="director" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
  Director: <span itemprop="name">James Cameron</span> (born <span itemprop="birthDate">August 16, 1954)</span>
  </div>
  <span itemprop="genre">Science fiction</span>
  <a href="../movies/avatar-theatrical-trailer.html" itemprop="trailer">Trailer</a>
</div>
